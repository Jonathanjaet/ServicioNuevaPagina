1st International Conference

November 9-14 2025 México

WELCOME!

The 1st International Conference on EMPOWER Women in MOFs and Beyond is aimed to enhance the visibility and recognition of the contributions of female scientist in the field of Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs) and similar porous hybrid materials, encouraging women to pursue their interests in science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM) and a career in academia. The ambition of the conference is to unify women in science: undergraduate students, postgraduate students, PhD students, postdoctoral researchers and established researchers, giving all participants the tools to establish an engaging and close-knit community of mentorship for young scientists gaining female role models in their own field and promoting women’s sense of belonging in STEM and academia. The source of inspiration of this first topical conference within this field arises from the main goal of promoting women researching within the field of MOFs and similar materials, striving to be a turning point in the history of this field. For this reason, all talks will be presented by women, thus promoting the broad visibility of their work. The audience will be of mixed gender, encouraging diversity in an inclusive forum for scientific discussions. Conference activities will include networking, scientific workshops and round-tables, supporting active communication. Additionally, the conference will host activities related to spreading awareness regarding gender equity and equality, with the aim of working toward a more diverse environment within the STEM fields. The mail goal of this conference is to promote women researching within the field of MOFs and similar porous hybrid materials, to unify the scientific community and to raise awareness of the need of gender equity and equality in the sciences. The conference wishes to promote an inclusive forum in which all participants of all genders are welcome. Overcoming gender inequality requires an ongoing open dialogue between all researchers of all genders, coupled to dedicated efforts in counteracting gender bias in the broader community. Although the term “women” is used in the context of this conference, we invite and welcome all attendees who identify as women, transgender, agender, genderqueer, non-binary, and Two-Spirit.

BACKGROUND

While gender parity in the scientific community has experienced some improvement over the last several decades, women still constitute a minority in science with 33.3% of the world’s researchers being women in 2018, according to the UNESCO Science Report (UNESCO, 2021). The consensus among the participating countries in the UNESCO STEM and Gender Advancement (SAGA) project is that there is an decreasing proportion of female researchers with increasing advancement to more senior career levels (UNESCO, 2021). Some examples of yet remaining issues, presented in the UNESCO Science Report, are economic inequalities, lower representation of women’s contributions in high-profile journals, lower citation levels of female-authored publications, funding disparities, and tougher standards for women in tenure evaluations, job applications and in the peer-review process (UNESCO, 2021). Women are also less often given plenary and key note presentations than men, thus reducing their visibility and recognition of their work (UNESCO, 2021). Overcoming the mentioned persistent challenges requires an ongoing open dialogue between all researchers, coupled to dedicated efforts that promote the scientific contributions of female researchers, building strong mentorship networks among female researchers and trainees, and actively counteracting gender bias in science. There is a growing global awareness of the benefits of gender parity, as well as having a diverse workforce, in terms of work efficiency and economic prosperity, and is considered an important element in tackling the imminent future challenges our planet and society face including Climate Change and its social, economic and environmental consequences, global health and future pandemics as well as enabling a positive transformation in the ongoing digital revolution (UNESCO, 2021). The aim of this conference is to enhance the visibility and recognition of the contributions of female scientist in the field of Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs) and similar porous hybrid materials, encouraging women to pursue their interests in science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM) and a career in academia by establishing a community of mentorship for young scientists gaining female role models in their own field and promoting women’s sense of belonging in STEM and academia. In addition, we also would like to emphasize the need of raising the awareness for all kinds of challenges women scientists face daily in academia, reporting from the broader community of the unsolved issues to the resulting consequences of the gender inequality in our society.

PROGRAM

With the aim of reaching a larger audience and participation, both nationally within Mexico and internationally, the conference is held at two locations in Mexico: Mexico City, the epicenter for the MOF community in Mexico and, Merida, a hub for Mexican culture, history, and nature. The two parts of the conference will have distinct agendas within the scope of the conference and options for participants to participate in both or one of the two sections. The Young Researchers Symposium in Mexico City will be focused on young women within the area of MOFs, COFs and similar materials will be the first “ice-breaker scenario”, with the goal to promote interaction, collaboration, networking, as well as mentoring, establishing a unified community of women in the area. The second part of the conference has been designed to engage participants in workshops and poster sessions in an informal and welcoming atmosphere, in the city of Merida, on the Yucatan peninsula of Mexico.
